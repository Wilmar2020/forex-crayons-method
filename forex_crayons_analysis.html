The Lead/Lag Principle

In the Crayon Method, the four colors don't move together. One leads. The others follow. The gap between the leader and the followers — measured in real price terms — is the trade opportunity.

When one color moves 21 pips in a direction while the others are 21 pips behind, the market is telling you exactly how much follow-through is left. Not a guess. Not a pattern. A live measurement of the forces already in motion.

The instrument that moves first reveals institutional intent. The instrument that moves last shows you the destination. The gap between them is your edge.

Traditional analysis asks: "Did price break the level?" The Crayon Method asks: "Is the book already positioned for this move, and who is leading?" Those are fundamentally different questions — and they produce fundamentally different results.

Found Fixed Defect 01 — The Moving Floor

The sweep confirmation system — the mechanism that identifies institutional stop runs before the real move begins — was calculating its reference level on every single bar. As price moved, the floor moved with it. Confirmation was mathematically impossible. The signal was coded correctly. It could never trigger.

The fix: The reference level is now frozen the instant the sweep begins. It locks. It does not move. Confirmation fires when price closes back through that exact locked level — not a moving target that chases price into oblivion.

Found Fixed Defect 02 — The Blind Parity Zone

The parity zone — the most critical price level in the entire system, where the four data streams converge and the market makes its decision — was using a fixed pip buffer regardless of timeframe or live market conditions. On a 15-minute chart with a 75-pip spread between key levels, a 10-pip zone is statistically invisible. The zone that governs stop placement, trade management and exit decisions was never activating correctly.

The fix: The buffer now breathes dynamically with the live market spread, scaled by a timeframe coefficient confirmed through live testing. Tighter on lower timeframes, wider on higher timeframes. The zone is always proportional to the current market — never fixed, never blind.

Found Fixed Defect 03 — The Stop Architecture

Because the first two defects prevented entries from firing, the stop management system — trailing stops, parity stops, manipulation exits — was built on a foundation that could never be reached. Three competing exit commands were fighting each other, the last one registered winning regardless of logic. Risk management was architecturally broken at the execution level.

The fix: A single active stop per side, cascading cleanly through three stages — entry stop, trail stop, parity stop. One command. No conflicts. The exit system now hands off between stages the way the method always intended.
